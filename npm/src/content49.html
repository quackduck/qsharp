We begin in the same state as the previous excercise: $$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \otimes \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = |0\rangle \otimes |0\rangle$$

The goal state can be separated as follows: $$ \frac{1}{\sqrt2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \otimes \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = |0\rangle \otimes \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\big(|0\rangle - |1\rangle\big)$$

This means that the first qubit is already in the state we want it to be, but the second qubit needs to be transformed from the $ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} $ into $ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$ state.

First, we apply the X gate to the second qubit; this performs the following transformation: $$ X |0\rangle = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = |1\rangle $$

Second, we apply the H gate to the second qubit; this transforms its state into the desired one: $$ H|1\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$$
